                            TARGET
---------------------------------------------------------------------
The Antennae Galaxies (NGC 4038 & NGC 4039)
Distance: approximately 71.75 Mly
Constellation: Corvus

The Antennae Galaxies are a pair of interacting galaxies, undergoing a galactic collision.
Located in the NGC 4038 group, these two galaxies are known as the 
Antennae Galaxies because the two long tails of stars, gas and dust ejected from the galaxies 
resemble an insect's antennae. The tails and a relatively young collection of massive globular
clusters are a result of the collision.
The nuclei of the two galaxies are joining to become one giant galaxy.
